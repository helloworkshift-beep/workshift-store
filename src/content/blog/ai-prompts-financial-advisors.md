# AI Prompts for Financial Advisors: Write Better Client Communications in Less Time

Financial advisors spend hours writing — quarterly reviews, prospect emails, market commentary, planning summaries. AI can handle the first draft of all of it. These prompts give you the structure.

## Client Communication Prompts

### Quarterly Portfolio Review Email

```
Write a quarterly portfolio review email for [CLIENT NAME].
Portfolio return: [X%]. Benchmark: [Y%]. Allocation: [CONSERVATIVE/MODERATE/AGGRESSIVE].
Key market events this quarter: [2-3 BULLET POINTS].
Tone: calm, reassuring, forward-looking. No jargon. Under 250 words.
Avoid: specific predictions, performance guarantees.
```

### Market Volatility Reassurance Email

```
Write a client email addressing recent market volatility — markets down [X%] this [PERIOD].
Client profile: [BRIEF — e.g., "retired couple, moderate risk, 15-year horizon"].
Goal: acknowledge the concern, reinforce the long-term strategy, avoid dismissing emotions.
Tone: confident but empathetic. Under 200 words. No specific predictions.
```

## Prospect Outreach Prompts

### Cold Outreach Email

```
Write an introductory email to [PROSPECT NAME], a [PROFESSION] who likely has [FINANCIAL SITUATION — e.g., "significant equity comp, no financial plan"].
My practice focuses on: [SPECIALTY — e.g., "equity comp planning for tech professionals"].
Goal: a 20-minute intro call, not a pitch. Sound like a peer, not a salesperson.
Under 120 words.
```

## Planning and Summary Prompts

### Financial Plan Executive Summary

```
Write an executive summary of a financial plan for [CLIENT NAME / COUPLE].
Their situation: [BRIEF — ages, income, assets, goals].
Key recommendations: [LIST 3-4].
Time horizon: [YEARS].
Translate complex concepts into plain English. No jargon.
This summary goes at the front of the full plan document. Under 400 words.
```

## Newsletter and Thought Leadership

### Monthly Client Newsletter Intro

```
Write the opening section of a monthly client newsletter for [MONTH].
Key market theme this month: [THEME].
My take / insight to share: [YOUR PERSPECTIVE].
Tone: informed, approachable — like writing to a smart friend, not a compliance document.
Under 200 words. End with a teaser for what's in the rest of the newsletter.
```

## The Compliance Caveat

These prompts help you draft faster — but always review output for compliance with your firm's policies and applicable regulations. AI doesn't know your specific client's situation the way you do, and it doesn't know your compliance requirements. Use it as a first draft, not a final answer.
