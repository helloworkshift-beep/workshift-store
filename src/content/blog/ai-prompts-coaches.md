# AI Prompts for Coaches: Scale Your Impact Without Burning Out

Coaching is deeply personal work. But building and running a coaching practice involves a lot of writing — proposals, session frameworks, follow-up emails, course content, social media, and client communication.

AI can handle most of that writing load, freeing you to do the work that actually requires your presence.

## What Can AI Help Coaches With?

- Discovery call frameworks and intake questionnaires
- Session prep and debrief templates
- Client proposals and program descriptions
- Email sequences for new and prospective clients
- Social media content and thought leadership
- Course outlines and workshop frameworks
- Journaling prompts and homework assignments for clients
- Testimonial and case study writing

## The Best AI Prompts for Coaches

### How do I write a coaching program proposal?

**Prompt:**
> "Write a coaching program proposal for a prospective client. My coaching focus: [YOUR SPECIALTY — e.g., executive coaching, life coaching, career transition, health coaching]. Program structure: [LENGTH — e.g., 3-month program with biweekly sessions]. What the client gets: [DELIVERABLES AND OUTCOMES]. Investment: [PRICE — I'll add the specifics]. Tone: warm and human, not corporate. Lead with transformation, not logistics."

### How do I create a client intake questionnaire?

**Prompt:**
> "Create a client intake questionnaire for a [COACHING TYPE] practice. Include questions covering: current situation and challenges, goals and desired outcomes, past experience with coaching, what success looks like to them, logistics (availability, communication preferences), and any concerns or questions before starting. 10-15 questions total. Mix of open-ended and structured."

### How do I write follow-up messages after coaching sessions?

**Prompt:**
> "Write a post-session follow-up email template for a coaching client. Include: brief acknowledgment of what was covered, key insight or reflection prompt, their homework or commitment for the next session, and an encouraging close. Warm, personal tone. Under 150 words."

### How do I write journaling prompts for coaching clients?

**Prompt:**
> "Create 10 powerful journaling prompts for a client who is working on [THEME — e.g., career clarity, confidence, life transitions, leadership development]. Each prompt should invite reflection and honest self-inquiry. Avoid generic self-help language. Make them feel personally chosen."

### How do I write a discovery call framework?

**Prompt:**
> "Design a 45-minute discovery call framework for a [COACHING TYPE] practice. Include: opening and rapport building (5 min), understanding their situation (10 min), exploring their goals (10 min), identifying what's blocking them (10 min), presenting your approach (5 min), and next steps/close (5 min). Add 2-3 key questions for each section."

### How can AI help me write social media content as a coach?

**Prompt:**
> "Write 5 LinkedIn posts for a [COACHING SPECIALTY] coach. Each post should share one short insight or lesson that demonstrates expertise and builds trust with potential clients. No fluff, no motivational posters. Real, specific, useful. Each post: 3-4 short paragraphs, ends with a reflective question."

## More High-Value Prompts for Coaches

**For testimonial writing:**
> "Based on this client feedback, write a polished testimonial for my coaching website: [PASTE CLIENT'S WORDS]. Keep their voice but improve structure and clarity. Keep it under 100 words. Format as a quote with their first name and role."

**For workshop design:**
> "Design a 3-hour workshop outline for [TARGET AUDIENCE] on [TOPIC]. Include: learning objectives, activities/exercises for each section, facilitation notes, and a closing integration activity. I want participants to leave with both insight and a concrete next step."

**For email nurture sequences:**
> "Write a 5-email welcome sequence for someone who joined my email list. I'm a [COACHING TYPE] coach. The sequence should: introduce me and my approach, share one quick win, tell a relevant client story, introduce my signature program, and invite a discovery call. Warm and human throughout — no salesy hype."

**For course content outlines:**
> "Create a course outline for an online program on [TOPIC] designed for [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Include: 5-6 modules, 3-4 lessons per module, learning objectives for each module, and a brief description of what each lesson covers. The course should take someone from [STARTING STATE] to [DESIRED OUTCOME]."

## AI as a Leverage Tool, Not a Replacement

Coaching is about human presence, deep listening, and intuitive response. No AI replaces that.

But the business of coaching — the writing, the frameworks, the content — is where most coaches lose time they'd rather spend with clients. That's where AI pays for itself immediately.
