# AI Prompts for Sales Reps: Templates for Every Stage of the Pipeline

The blank-screen problem is real in sales. You know what you want to say — you just need help saying it fast, in the right way, for the right prospect. These prompts cut the writing time by 80%.

## Cold Outreach Prompts

### Cold Email (Problem-First)

```
Act as a B2B sales copywriter. Write a cold email to [PROSPECT NAME], [TITLE] at [COMPANY].
They likely struggle with [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT].
My product, [PRODUCT], helps [TARGET PERSONA] [VALUE PROP].
Under 100 words. One clear CTA. No buzzwords. Sound human.
```

### LinkedIn Connection Request

```
Write a LinkedIn connection request to [PROSPECT NAME] at [COMPANY].
Context: [WHY YOU'RE REACHING OUT — e.g., "saw their post about X", "we work with similar companies"].
Keep it under 300 characters. No pitch. Just a reason to connect.
```

## Discovery and Qualification Prompts

### Discovery Call Prep

```
Help me prepare for a discovery call with [COMPANY NAME].
Their industry: [INDUSTRY]. Size: [EMPLOYEES/REVENUE].
What I know about them: [ANY CONTEXT].
Generate: 5 open-ended pain discovery questions, 3 qualification questions (budget/authority/timeline), 
and 2 questions that set up our differentiators naturally.
```

## Objection Handling Prompts

### "We Already Have a Vendor" Response

```
Write a response to: "We already use [COMPETITOR] for this."
My product: [PRODUCT]. Key differentiators vs [COMPETITOR]: [LIST].
Goal: keep the conversation open, don't badmouth them.
Conversational tone, 3-4 sentences max.
```

### "Too Expensive" Response

```
Write a response to a price objection: "That's more than we were expecting to spend."
Product: [PRODUCT]. Price: [$AMOUNT]. What it replaces or saves: [CONTEXT].
Reframe around ROI without being defensive. Under 100 words.
```

## Follow-Up Prompts

### Post-Demo Follow-Up Email

```
Write a follow-up email after a demo with [PROSPECT NAME] at [COMPANY].
What they liked: [POSITIVES]. Their concern: [MAIN OBJECTION/QUESTION].
Next step we agreed on: [NEXT STEP]. 
Warm, professional tone. Under 150 words. Include a clear subject line.
```

## Proposal and Closing Prompts

### Executive Summary for Proposal

```
Write an executive summary for a sales proposal to [COMPANY NAME].
Their problem: [PROBLEM]. Our solution: [SOLUTION]. 
Key outcomes they care about: [OUTCOMES]. Investment: [$AMOUNT].
Audience: [C-LEVEL / VP — non-technical decision maker]. 
Focus on business outcomes, not features. Under 200 words.
```

## The Real Advantage

These prompts don't replace your sales judgment — they replace the blank-screen paralysis. You still make the strategic call on what to say. The AI just executes it faster and more cleanly than starting from scratch.
