# AI Prompts for Architects: Write Faster, Present Better, Win More Projects

Architecture is a visual discipline. But winning projects, managing clients, and running a practice requires a surprising amount of writing — proposals, project narratives, specification notes, client emails, and presentations.

AI can handle most of that writing. Here's how.

## What Can AI Help Architects With?

- Project proposals and design narratives
- Client presentations and meeting summaries
- Specification document outlines
- Project scope descriptions for permits and submissions
- Marketing content: firm bio, portfolio descriptions, awards submissions
- Client communications during design and construction
- RFI and submittal tracking summaries
- Staff training and project management documentation

## The Best AI Prompts for Architects

### How do I write a design narrative with AI?

**Prompt:**
> "Write a design narrative for an architecture project with the following details: Project type: [TYPE — residential/commercial/institutional]. Location: [CITY/CONTEXT]. Design concept: [YOUR CORE IDEA IN 1-2 SENTENCES]. Key design moves: [LIST 3-5]. Materials/palette: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. Tone: professional and evocative, suitable for a jury presentation or client booklet. Length: 300-400 words."

### How do I write a project proposal for a potential architecture client?

**Prompt:**
> "Write a proposal letter for an architecture commission. Client: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. Project: [SCOPE]. My firm's relevant experience: [LIST 2-3 RELEVANT PROJECTS OR CREDENTIALS]. Proposed approach/phases: [LIST]. Tone: confident, design-forward, collaborative. Length: one page. I'll add fees separately."

### How can AI help me write award submission entries?

**Prompt:**
> "Write an awards submission narrative for [PROJECT NAME]. Project type: [TYPE]. Key design challenges: [LIST]. Design response: [HOW YOU ADDRESSED THEM]. Notable outcomes: [WHAT MAKES IT AWARD-WORTHY — sustainability, innovation, community impact, etc.]. Length: [WORD LIMIT]. Tone: compelling, precise, avoids clichéd architectural language."

### How do I communicate construction issues to clients clearly?

**Prompt:**
> "Write a professional email to a client explaining the following construction issue: [DESCRIBE THE ISSUE]. Cause: [WHY IT HAPPENED]. Impact: [SCHEDULE/COST/QUALITY IMPACT]. Proposed resolution: [WHAT YOU RECOMMEND]. Tone: transparent and solution-focused. Avoid blaming anyone. Keep it under 250 words."

### How do I write a specification section outline?

**Prompt:**
> "Create an outline for a specification section for [SYSTEM OR MATERIAL — e.g., exterior glazing system, metal roofing, cast-in-place concrete]. Include standard CSI format headings: General, Products, Execution. List the key items that should appear under each heading for this system. I'll fill in the specific requirements."

### How do I write a firm bio for marketing?

**Prompt:**
> "Write a 150-word architecture firm bio for our website. Firm name: [NAME]. Founded: [YEAR]. Specialty: [TYPES OF PROJECTS]. Philosophy: [1-2 SENTENCES ON APPROACH]. Notable projects or credentials: [LIST]. Tone: professional but human — we want to attract the right clients, not win a competition."

## More Useful Prompts

**For meeting summaries:**
> "Write a meeting summary from the following notes: [PASTE NOTES OR KEY POINTS]. Format: Date, Attendees, Key Discussions, Decisions Made, Action Items (with responsible party). Professional tone. I'll email this to the client."

**For project milestone emails:**
> "Write a project milestone update email to a residential client. Project: [NAME/TYPE]. Milestone reached: [E.G., DESIGN DEVELOPMENT COMPLETE]. What's next: [PHASE]. What I need from them: [DECISIONS, APPROVALS, INFORMATION]. Warm but professional tone."

**For portfolio descriptions:**
> "Write a 100-word portfolio project description for [PROJECT NAME]. Project type: [TYPE]. Location: [LOCATION]. Key design ideas: [2-3 POINTS]. Square footage: [SF]. Year completed: [YEAR]. Suitable for website portfolio or award submission."

## The Writing Load in Architecture

In a typical project, an architect produces: proposal letters, design narratives, client updates, consultant coordination emails, RFI responses, change order descriptions, closeout summaries, and follow-up correspondence. That's dozens of documents per project.

AI doesn't change your design thinking. It just removes the friction between your ideas and the page.
