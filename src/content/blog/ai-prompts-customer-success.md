# AI Prompts for Customer Success Managers: Retain More, Expand Faster

CSMs manage dozens of accounts while maintaining personalized communication. These prompts help you scale quality — so no account feels like a template.

## Onboarding Prompts

### Kickoff Meeting Agenda

```
Create a kickoff meeting agenda for [COMPANY NAME], a new customer using [PRODUCT].
Their primary goal: [GOAL]. Key stakeholders attending: [ROLES].
Meeting length: [X] minutes.
Include: introductions, success plan review, technical setup check, 
Q&A, and clear next steps with owners and dates.
```

## QBR Prompts

### QBR Executive Summary

```
Write a QBR executive summary for [COMPANY NAME].
Product: [PRODUCT]. Contract value: [ACV]. 
Usage highlights this quarter: [KEY METRICS].
Business outcomes achieved: [OUTCOMES — tie to their goals, not your features].
Open risks: [IF ANY].
Next quarter priorities: [LIST].
Audience: [VP/C-LEVEL]. Max 300 words. Lead with outcomes, not activity.
```

## Risk and Churn Prevention

### At-Risk Account Re-engagement

```
Write an email to re-engage [CONTACT NAME] at [COMPANY], an at-risk account.
Low usage for [X] weeks. Previous outreach: [X] touches, no response.
Their original goal when they signed: [GOAL].
Tone: direct but not desperate. Lead with value, not guilt. 
Offer a specific agenda for a quick call. Under 120 words.
```

### Executive Escalation Email

```
Write an escalation email to [EXECUTIVE NAME/TITLE] at [COMPANY].
Situation: [BRIEF — e.g., "implementation stalled, champion has gone quiet"].
What we've done to resolve: [LIST].
What I'm asking for: [SPECIFIC ASK — 20 min call, executive sponsor meeting].
Tone: peer-to-peer, not salesy. We have a shared interest in success.
Under 150 words.
```

## Expansion Prompts

### Upsell/Expansion Introduction

```
Write an expansion conversation opener to [CONTACT NAME] at [COMPANY].
Current contract: [PRODUCT/TIER]. Expansion opportunity: [PRODUCT/TIER].
Why now — trigger event: [TRIGGER — new hire, new use case, usage hitting limit].
Frame as a natural next step based on their success, not a sales call.
Under 200 words.
```

## Internal Documentation

### Account Health Summary

```
Write an internal account health summary for [COMPANY NAME].
Health score: [RED/YELLOW/GREEN]. 
Usage trend: [INCREASING/FLAT/DECLINING].
Key risks: [LIST]. Key strengths: [LIST].
Recommended actions for next 30 days: [LIST].
Format: structured bullet points for quick scanning by leadership.
```

## Scale Without Losing the Personal Touch

The paradox of CSM at scale: more accounts means less time per account, which risks making every interaction feel templated. Good prompts actually solve this — because they're fast enough to use for every account, but specific enough to sound personal when you fill in the details.
